To use the system below, you should be familiar with Players' Option: Skills and Powers rules. You do not need to own the book, you just have to know how the thing works.

The idea is as follows: instead of using classes, which restrict the abilities a player can have, players now have total freedom when creating their character. The rules allow for one to advance in Thac0 like a warrior and in hit points like a wizard (though that won't happen too often.)

Step By Step

  1. Determine what kind of character you wish to play. Picture what he looks like, his behaviour, general character, and his race.
  2. Use what ever method you like to generate his 6 (12) subability scores. I recommend top give the players 130-160 ability points, which they can freely divide among their sub-scores. Exceptional strength can be purchased for 1 point per category (by any character except halflings). Players may opt to save ability points. One ability point is then converted to 2 character points.
  3. Now, choose a race, and pick the abilities that are unique to this race as per the standard players' option rules (it is still not possible for an elf to have mining skills).
  4. Pick the abilities you want, working through all the options from the first to the last. If you read further, this means you start with class abilities, then kit abilities, and then proficiencies.
  5. Now, let your DM check if everything is right. As a DM, you are advised to make sure that raw combat power between the characters is about evenly matched. Also, rule out anything that doesn't fit to your world or your personal style. (Players, don't complain about this, you have choice enough already.)
  6. Write up your Character Sheet.
  7. Equip your character.
  8. Write down some notes about history and behaviour of you character.
  9. Done!

DMs Note: the rules are designed so that you can use them next to existing rules. For example, a player who only knows First Quest rules can still use these rules, and all other systems can be used together as well. However, there will be more diversity in characters if you'll only use these rules.

Introduction

In the standard system, you belong to a class and cannot change this class to another one. It is also not possible to combine the classes freely. While you can play a fighter/mage, this costs a lot: you require double xp and cannot cast spells while in armor. Wouldn't it be better if you could play a character who is a good fighter, but not as good as a real one, and you would be able to use some spells, but not as good as a real wizard?

In this new system, the boundaries between the classes are shifted. You are free to choose any ability you wish. For example, playing a wizard who gains 1d12 hp per level is now possible. The idea is still the same: you use CPs to purchase abilities.

After you have determined your ability scores and race, you are ready to select all other abilities. While there are no abilities you cannot select separately, some will be illogical. Try to build a character in a logical way. Don't try to create a character that is all-powerful. Keep in style! (for example, it is very strange for a character to be able to create magical items as a wizard, while being unable to use them!)

I'll be creating a character for a generic campaign world (lets say FR) as an example. It is assumed you have access to all players' option books.

The DM has told me that I have 155 points (like all others I play with) to divide among my subabilities. I cannot play drow, duergar, half-orcs or half-ogres. All other races are allowed. In addition, there is no limit to how much character points I can save from the racial point allowance.

First, I have to describe my character in a few sentences to the DM: My character is an eternal wanderer. I cannot stay in one place for too long, and have trouble finding friends. As such, I have learned skills that allow me to detail with most dangers of the road. In a way, I'm much like a bard. I will play a half-elf, for this description suits a half-elf.

This will be my scores: 

Stamina: 16            Muscle: 14              

Aim: 13                Balance: 15             

Health: 10             Fitness: 12             

Reason: 13             Knowledge: 10           

Intuition: 15          Willpower: 11           

Leadership: 12         Appearance: 14

I spent all the 155 points on the abilities, not saving any. Of the Half-Elf racial abilities, I only choose infravision, 60'(10 points, leaving 15.)

At this point, each player gains 100 Character Points, which can be spent on the following abilities. To these 100 points, add anything left from the score selection and from the racial abilities.

Abilities with shaded background are optional; your DM may not allow all (or any.)

That means 100+15=115 for me. Lets go shopping.

Choosing Primary Abilities

The first thing you'll have to do is select such basic things as the number of hit points you gain per level and Thac0.

Hit Points

If you do not spend any points here, you will receive 1d8 hit points per level, up to and including level 9. Starting at level 10, you'll only gain 1 hit point per level. Characters always get hit point bonuses from high constitution.

  • no hit point improvement (+40) This option gives 40 extra points, but the character will not gain extra hit points when he increases in level. (1d8 hit points at the beginning of the game, and no more, except from constitution.)
  • 1 hit point per level (+25) The character gains 1 hit point per level beyond first.
  • 1d4 hit points per level (+15) The character gains 1d4 hit points up to and including 9th (not 10th) level.
  • 1d6 hit points/level (+10)
  • 1d10 hit points/level (15)
  • 1d12 hit points/level (20)
  • 2d8 hit points/level (30)
  • no hit points after 9th level (+5)
  • 2 hp/lvl after 9th (5)
  • 3 hp/lvl after 9th (10)
  • 1d8 hp/lvl after 9th (25)

Thac0

If you do not spend any points here, your THAC0 will improve with one point every three levels (like a wizard).

Your highest Thac0 attainable will be 14.

  • no THAC0 improvement (+15) The characters' THAC0 will never improve beyond 20. (though bonuses from strength, specialisation and others still apply.)
  • 1/2 lvl THAC0 improvement (10) The characters' THAC0 improves with one point every two levels beyond first (like a thief).
  • 2/3 lvl THAC0 improvement (15)
  • 1/1 lvl THAC0 improvement (20)
  • 3/2 lvl THAC0 improvement (30)
  • 2/1 lvl THAC0 improvement (40)

Armor Allowed

Standard, a character is allowed to wear any armor and use any shield (though wizard spells can still not be used when wearing armor, and other rules like these still apply).

  • no armor allowed (+20) The character is not allowed to wear any armor at all. He cannot use shields.
  • armor up to ac 8 (leather armor) (+15)
  • armor up to ac 5 (chain mail) (+5)

Weapons Allowed

If you do not spend any points here, you will be able to use any weapon (limited by size, and optionally, strength), normally. The penalty for nonproficiency is -2.

  • no weapons allowed (+25) The character cannot become proficient with any weapon, though he may still use them. (using a wizards' nonproficiency penalty of -5).
  • wizard weapon selection (+15) (Use non-proficiency penalty of -5)
  • rogue weapon selection (+10) (use rogue penalty for nonproficiency)
  • priest weapon selection (+10)
  • weapon specialisation (10) Standard, you are not allowed to gain weapon specialisation or mastery. (though you can gain familiarity and expertise). With this ability you can gain specialisation. (note this ability appears slightly different below). If you select this ability, you can also buy weapon proficiencies for 2 CPs a piece.

Saving Throws

A character should choose the saving throw table that is most appropriate for his character.

  • no saving throw improvement (+15) If you choose this ability, you will have the saving throws of a 0th-level human for ever after.

Magical Items and Spells

Standard, a character can only use magical items like a warrior. All other items are barred to him.

  • No Magical Item Use (+25) If a character choose this ability, he either choose to use magical items, or is unable to for a mysterious reason. (If a character already has a problem using magical items, like a dwarf, he only gains 10 points for this disability.)
  • Rogue Magical Item Use (5)
  • Priest Magical Item Use (10)
  • Wizard Magical Item Use (10)

Psionic Abilities

These abilities can only be chosen if psionics are allowed in the campaign.

A character who wishes access to any of these abilities has to spend 10 points.

  • Attack Specialisation (10): The psionicist is good at using psionicist attacks, gaining a +1 bonus on MTHAC0 rolls vs. Character MACs and doing an extra +2 bonus on the PSP damage. Also, the Psionicist gains extra psionic attacks faster as normal:
    Level: 1-6 7-12 13+
    # of attacks: 3/2 2/1 5/2
  • Bonus PSPs (5): The psionicist gains an extra PSP at each level of experience.
  • Charm Resistance (10): The Psionicist gains a +2 on Saving Throws vs. charm-related spells.
  • Contact (3): The psionicist can participate in psionic combat.
  • Detect Psionics (10): The psionicist can detect psionic activity (the expenditure of PSPs) in a path 10 feet wide and 60 feet long. Chance of Success is 10%, plus the psionicists wisdom score and his level.
  • Dis/Sci/Dev Progression (30): The Psionicist gains Disciplines, Sciences and Devotions as normal.
  • Fast Recovery (10): The psionicist recovers his PSPs faster, at a rate of 1/6 of his total per hour.
  • followers (5/10): The psionicist gains followers as described in Skills and Powers. By spending 10 CPs, he gains these followers at 2nd level.
  • Improved MTHAC0 (10): The Psionicist gains a +2 bonus on his MTHAC0.
  • MTHAC0 (15): The Psionicst has the MTHAC0 of a psionicist.
  • Wild Talent MTHAC0 (10): The Psionicsit has the MTHAC0 of a wild talent.
  • Persistent Power (10 +2 per point of maintenance cost): The Psionicist may select one Devotion he knows to have a persistent, permanent duration. He permanently looses the PSPs that this power costs. By concentrating, the wizard may activate the power and maintain it for as long as he continues to concentrate. For example, a persistent All-Round vision would cost 20 CPs and reduce the psionicist PSP total by 5, permanently.
  • Point Progression (35): The Psionicist gains Disciplines, Sciences and Devotions using the system described here: The psionicist gets disciplines as normal, and gains a number of points to spent on his sciences and devotions. A science costs 2 points, a devotion costs one point. The psionicist starts with 5 points at first level, and gains 1 ½ point every level afterwards.
    Masha, a 3rd lvl psionicist, uses this system to purchase her powers. She chooses Psychometabolism as a Prime Discipline, and Psychokinetism as a second Discipline. Now she has 8 (5+3) points to spent on sciences and disciplines. She could take 4 sciences and no devotions, or 1 science and 6 devotions. Or she could purchase 1 science and 6 devotions, which is what a psionicist normally has at 3rd level.
  • Preparation Time reduction (3/5): The initiative phase of the PCs psionic powers goes up by one phase. For 2 CPs, the psionicist may accelerate the powers from his primary discipline only. For 5 CPs, all powers are faster.
  • Psionicist PSPs (25): The psionicist gains PSPs as a psionicist.
  • Wild Talent PSPs (15): The psionicist gains PSPs as a wild talent.
  • Range Increase (5/7): All ranges from the psionicists primary discipline increase by 25% for 5 CPs or by 50% for 7 PSPs. This does not apply to powers with a range of personal or touch.

Other rules and clarifications

About Kits and Proficiencies

It is assumed that someone of the adventurer class can choose any proficiency and any kit. Weapon proficiencies cost 3 cp/proficiency, unless the character can specialise in a weapon. Note that to be able to use the warrior special attack specialisations and stuff you need to have the specialisation ability.

It is assumed players no longer gain CPs to spend on (non)weapon proficiencies. If you do wish to give your players these CPs, give them 6 for nonweapon proficiencies, and 6 for weapon proficiencies as well.

Buying Character Points

You may wish to 'buy' extra character points. This is possible, though it means a stiff penalty. You can gain extra CPs by taking on xp penalties, as shown in the table below.

Humans may not opt to use this rule. If you completely discard this rule, demihumans loose the advantage of multi-classing.

Creating New Abilities

It is easy for DMs to add new abilities. For example, you may wish to allow (dis)abilities from kits to be chosen as well. (I don't like kits, so I haven't made the effort.) Simply assign a character point cost to the ability.

As a player, you may wish to have an ability that isn't on the list. Ask your DM!

Creating New Classes

You may not wish to use these rules. That's just fine. But you can still use them to create new classes easy, and more quickly, too. Simply take a 100 points and buy the abilities. Write these down and you have a class. If you do create a class this way, it is best if you use the wizard or ranger xp table, instead of the one above. The extra xp penalty in this table is there because freedom of choice should somehow be penalised.

Delayed Ability Acquisition

As an optional rule, players can reduce the cost of an ability by choosing to delay it to a higher level. An ability that is not acquired until 3rd level is 1 point cheaper than the listed cost, an ability delayed to 5th level is 2 points cheaper, an ability delayed to 7th level is 3 points cheaper, an ability delayed to 9th level is 4 points cheaper, and an ability delayed to 11th level is 5 points cheaper. The minimum cost for an ability is 1 point.

Additional Ability Acquisition

As another optional rule, players can acquire class abilities as they rise in level, as they can choose proficiencies. For example, a player can choose to gain the weapon specialisation ability (10CPs) at 5th level.

However, characters cannot gain access to ability groups they previously did not have access to (if that rule is used). So, if the player did not choose access to the thieving group at the beginning, he cannot gain any abilities from that group later.

The cost fro selecting new abilities becomes more and more as the player gains levels. At levels 1 to 3, the cost is as normal. At levels 4-7, players pay 1 CP extra for each ability. At levels 8-9, they pay 2 CPs extra, at levels 10-11 this is 3 CPs, at levels 12-13, this is 4 CPs, at levels 14 and 15, this is 5 CPs. Beyond 15th level, players can no longer pursue abilities in this way.

Multi-classed characters

Playing a multi-class character is not possible anymore. The rules above give players a way to create a sort of multi-class character already.

Dual-classed characters

The rules for switching classes are not changed for humans. Simply give him 80 points again, and allow him to select his new abilities. Treat these as his new class. Note that it is not necessary to select the primary abilities again, so the points gained are decreased from 100 to 80.

Abilities from the High-Level Campaign handbook

I have not worked out new rules for these abilities. I suggest you use them as they are there, allowing characters only those abilities that are appropriate for them.

Abilities from other sources

If you wish to use other abilities, that means you have to do some work. Rules for characters from the Dark Sun setting are downloadable from my DS site.
