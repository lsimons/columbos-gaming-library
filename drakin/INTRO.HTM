An Introduction to the World of Drakin

The idea for the World of Drakin has slowly grown within me from the moment I acquired the DMG. But I decided my project idea was way to hard for me as a starting DM. And so I made other worlds. One was based on Tolkien's Lord of the Rings, in another one I used ideas from "Wizard of Earthsea".

After more worlds and much learning, I purchased the Dark Sun box, for only one reason: it had dragons in command. I have DMed several Dark Sun campaigns, and I still play with it at times.

It was when my experienced players started asking me for some high-level intrigue and wars, that my original idea for Drakin was ready for development. I started working on the world, while experimenting with high-level play in an old world, where I was free to try out what worked and what did not, because it didn't matter if I screwed it up.

When I asked my players to roll up characters for a new world, I had written about 75 pages of typed A4s of information on the world. After a few adventures that got the players drawn into the world, I started working on what I would later name "The Quest Series".

The Dragon Kings campaign setting takes place in the Known World, a small area, about 1000 times 700 miles. This area is inhabited by all the common adnd races. Humans are the dominant race, with the goblinoids not far behind. The other races are less prevalent and most are only commonly found in restricted areas. All these races worship the same god, though under different names and with different religions. The most powerful religion is that of the Church. Most people are members of the Church, and obey the priests of the Church.

This Church has its own nation, called the Land of Faith. Here, the religious monarchy is in control of almost anything. Besides the Land of Faith, almost half of all the land in other countries is the property of the Church.

The other nations are all ruled by powerful dragons. These are really wizards who have successfully cast a Dragon Metamorphosis spell. This spell connects them to either the positive or negative energy plane, and thus grants them many magical powers, as well as immortality. Their alignments are either totally good or utter evil. The dragons rule their countries in various ways. Most are simple dictators, whilst others let the nobles of the world manage government.

These noble families are numerous and often still very powerful. Before the rise of the dragons, they were absolute rulers. These nobles are essentially like the nobles of medieval europe, with a dragon as an all-powerful king ruling them. A fragile military and political balance exists between Church, dragons and nobles.

The Known World is bordered by a large sea in the west and south. To he north there are the Endless Swamps, inhabited by the dangerous warrior creatures known as klingons. These klingons are a continous threat to the Known World, like orcs are in standard adnd to the human nations.
To the east, there is a range of high mountains inhabited by dangerous creatures. Beyond that, there is a vast desert that's almost uninhabitable. Because of these natural borders, the people of the Known World have not explored much of Drakin.

The starting adventure spot for the Dragon Kings campaign is the city of DragonMountain. This city-state is the only nation that is ruled by normal humans. This unique position, together with its great trading position, has made this city one where activity never stops, as well as a place where thieves and other dangers are numerous.
