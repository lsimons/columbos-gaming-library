Dungeon Master Aids

'You people there on your planet Terra explain your universe, your plane by a set of rules you call "physical laws", or something. Other planes, other planets have their own ways of explaining their universe.
'All these different worlds, with all their different "laws" can be explained using what is known as the adnd game system. Yet every planet needs it own modifications for this system to work. And the system is far from perfect. So we change the laws, we change the system. And then there's a new planet, and there are new laws. And so the cycle goes on.'

What I see as the best part of DMing is being the Dungeon Master. I also see it as the hardest part. I think it is good for every DM, no matter how much experience he has, to listen to the ideas of others.
Here are some of these ideas.
Essay on Successful ADND campaigns By Leo Simons
This document differs from most other DM guides in that it gives much more concrete information. A step-by-step guide to designing you own campaign world, and a guide to writing good adventures.
ASCII version HTML version RTF version
Essay on Successful ADND campaigns part II: dungeons By Leo Simons
This document is the second part in the "essay" series. Where the forst guide dealt with building worlds and adventures, this one gives advice on making a dungeon.
no ASCII version HTML version RTF version
Essay on Successful ADND campaigns part III: cities By Leo Simons
This is the third part in the "essay" series. The logical step after world, adventure and dungeon creation was city creation. This document gives simple clear advice and tips on how to make a large city.
no ASCII version HTML version RTF version
Onions.thread By various people
This document really gives good advice on how to write more complex adventures. It's a compilation of messages on the Compuserve Roleplaying Games Forum. Really, you should check this out.
ASCII version HTML version RTF version
Guide to World Design By Unknown
This document details the two main approaches when designing a gaming world: bottom-up and top-down. Some good ideas. Clearly well-thought about.
